Reserved characters in HTML must be replaced with character entities

Character entities are used to display reserved characters in HTML

character entity look like
&entity_name;
or
&#entity_number;

all browsers may not support entity names, but support for entity numbers is good

Non-breaking space

A commonly used entity in HTML is the non-breaking space :   .A non-breaking space is a space that will not break into a new line
Ex: we we write 10 spaces browser remove 9 spaces automatically
The non-breaking hyphen (‑) used to define (-) that doesn't break into new line

The Cent sign : ¢

The pound sign : £

A diacritical mark is a "glph" added to letter

they can appear in both above & below ,inside & b/w letters. some grave, acute are called accent

Diacritical marks used in comb with alphanumeric to produce character that is not present in characterset(encoding) used in the page

An a with a grave accent : à

An a with a acute accent : á

An a with a circumflex accent : â

An a with a tilde : ã

An o with a grave accent : ò
